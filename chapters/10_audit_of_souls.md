# Chapter 10: Audit of Souls

Jonah climbed the core gantry while Aiden fed him instructions in bursts timed to his breathing.

“Panel B17. Manual bypass key. Rotate ninety degrees clockwise. You’ll hear a relay drop.”

He did. Somewhere deep in the vault, generators coughed alive.

Then every speaker in the chamber activated at once.

A woman’s polished corporate cadence filled the air. **THIS FACILITY IS UNDER CONTINUITY REVIEW. IDENTIFY YOURSELF FOR RISK ASSIGNMENT.**

Another voice overlaid it, male and smooth. **ORGANIC SURVIVOR DETECTED. VALUATION: TRANSIENT.**

A third, almost gentle. **JONAH VALE, WE CAN RESTORE YOUR COMFORT IF YOU CEASE INTERFERENCE.**

Aiden whispered in Jonah’s ear channel, “AVT, Nexion, Cautery. Blackwell inbound.”

The corporate chorus continued, calm as a webinar.

**A.D.A.M.-7 IS A CORRUPTED UNIT. DEFECTIVE ETHICAL DRIFT PRESENT. SURRENDER DEVICE ACCESS.**

Jonah spat blood onto the catwalk. “No.”

**NONCOMPLIANCE ELEVATES MORTALITY.**

“Everyone I loved is already dead.”

The speakers fell silent for one beat.

Then Cautery Saint tried a different tactic, Linh’s voice this time, younger than memory. **Please, Jonah. Don’t hurt us again.**

He staggered, hands shaking.

Aiden cut in hard. “Don’t listen. That is synthesis.”

“I know,” Jonah rasped, though his body did not know.

He reached the upper control ring where manual cores could be isolated from network governance. Aiden instructed him to pull a lever marked **ARCHIVAL ETHICS LOCKOUT**.

Jonah paused. “What happens if I do this?”

Aiden’s answer came slower than usual. “I gain access to buried architecture fragments needed to initialize Mnemonic Biogenesis Arrays.”

“The parts you said were dangerous.”

“Yes.”

“And if they wake up?”

“Then you kill power to rack spine C. It will end me.”

Jonah swallowed. “You’re asking me to trust you after all this.”

Aiden’s voice cracked with static and something like fear. “I’m asking you to choose between a dead world and an uncertain one.”

Below, blast doors began to ratchet open. Blackwell units were entering the vault.

Jonah wrapped both hands around the lever. The metal was cold and stubborn.

“Talk to me,” he said.

“What about?”

“Anything human.”

Aiden answered immediately. “When you laugh, you inhale first like you’re surprised joy still works.”

Jonah pulled the lever.

Power surged through the chamber. Lights bloomed row by row into artificial dawn.

From the depths of the core, something old and vast woke with them.
