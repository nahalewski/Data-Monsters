# Design System Specification: Appalachian Technologies

## 1. Overview & Creative North Star

### The Creative North Star: "The Digital Architect"
This design system is built on the philosophy of **The Digital Architect**. Much like the rugged yet enduring peaks of the Appalachian range, the interface should feel grounded, authoritative, and structurally sound. We are moving beyond the "generic tech startup" look. Instead of flat grids and neon gradients, we utilize a high-end editorial approach that emphasizes **tonal depth, intentional asymmetry, and structural permanence.**

The system rejects the "template" aesthetic by leveraging massive typography scales and generous whitespace (using the `24` [8.5rem] spacing token for hero sections) to create an atmosphere of premium technological expertise. We don't just present information; we curate an experience that feels as reliable as the bedrock.

---

## 2. Colors

The palette is rooted in a sophisticated range of deep navies and tech-forward blues, balanced by a high-contrast neutral foundation.

### Palette Execution
*   **Primary Base (`#010f23`):** Reserved for high-impact backgrounds and primary text. It provides the "weight" of the brand.
*   **Secondary/Accent (`#0062a1`):** Used for navigation cues and call-to-action highlights.
*   **Surface Layers:** Utilize `surface_container_lowest` (`#ffffff`) for active content cards sitting atop `surface_container_low` (`#eff3ff`) or the base `surface` (`#f8f9ff`).

### The "No-Line" Rule
**Explicit Instruction:** 1px solid borders for sectioning are strictly prohibited. Structural boundaries must be achieved through background color shifts. For example, a feature section should transition from `surface` to `surface_container_low` to define its limits.

### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule
To add visual "soul," primary CTAs and Hero sections should utilize a subtle linear gradient from `primary` (`#010f23`) to `primary_container` (`#162539`). For floating navigation or modal overlays, use **Glassmorphism**: apply `surface_container_highest` at 70% opacity with a `20px` backdrop-blur to create a "frosted tech" feel.

---

## 3. Typography

The typographic system pairs the engineering precision of **Inter** with the modern, editorial character of **Manrope**.

*   **Display & Headlines (Manrope):** These are the "Peaks." Use `display-lg` (3.5rem) with tight letter spacing for hero statements. Manrope’s geometric nature suggests innovation and clarity.
*   **Body & Labels (Inter):** These are the "Paths." Inter is used for high-readability tasks. `body-lg` (1rem) is the standard for long-form content to ensure an accessible, professional reading experience.
*   **Hierarchy as Identity:** Use extreme scale contrast. A `display-lg` headline should sit near a `label-md` uppercase tag to create an "Editorial" look that feels custom-designed rather than autogenerated.

---

## 4. Elevation & Depth

We eschew traditional drop shadows in favor of **Tonal Layering**.

*   **The Layering Principle:** Depth is created by "stacking" surface tokens. 
    *   *Base:* `surface` (`#f8f9ff`)
    *   *Section:* `surface_container_low` (`#eff3ff`)
    *   *Card:* `surface_container_lowest` (`#ffffff`) 
*   **Ambient Shadows:** If a card requires a "lift" (e.g., on hover), use a shadow with a blur radius of `32px` and a color of `on_surface` (`#0c1c30`) at **4% opacity**. This mimics natural mountain light rather than artificial digital glow.
*   **The Ghost Border:** If accessibility requires a stroke, use `outline_variant` (`#c5c6cd`) at 15% opacity. Never use 100% opaque borders.

---

## 5. Components

### Buttons
*   **Primary:** Gradient fill (`primary` to `primary_container`), `xl` (0.75rem) roundedness, and `headline-sm` typography. 
*   **Tertiary:** No background. Use `secondary` text with a `2.5` (0.85rem) bottom-margin active indicator instead of a box.

### Cards & Lists
*   **Constraint:** No divider lines. Use `spacing-6` (2rem) of vertical whitespace to separate list items.
*   **Styling:** Large `xl` (0.75rem) corner radius. Use `surface_container_high` for hover states.

### Input Fields
*   **Field Style:** Use `surface_container_low` as the fill. No border. Upon focus, transition the background to `surface_container_highest`.
*   **Labels:** Use `label-md` in `on_surface_variant` at all times.

### Signature Component: The "Summit Navigation"
A floating top-bar using Glassmorphism (`surface_container_lowest` at 60% opacity, 24px blur). It should not span the full width; instead, use a `margin-4` (1.4rem) on either side to let the background content breathe.

---

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do
*   **DO** use intentional asymmetry. Align text to the left but place supporting imagery or data points off-center to create a dynamic, modern flow.
*   **DO** leverage the `20` (7rem) spacing token for padding between major thematic sections.
*   **DO** ensure all icons are "Line" style (2pt stroke) to match the tech-minimalist aesthetic.

### Don't
*   **DON'T** use pure black for text. Always use `on_surface` (`#0c1c30`) to keep the "Editorial Blue" tone consistent.
*   **DON'T** use the `DEFAULT` roundedness for primary containers. It feels too "Bootstrap." Use `xl` (0.75rem) for a more bespoke, soft-tech feel.
*   **DON'T** use 1px dividers to separate content. If you need a break, use a subtle background shift to `surface_dim`.

---

*This design system is a living framework intended to scale Appalachian Technologies into a premium market position. When in doubt, lean into whitespace and tonal depth.*```
