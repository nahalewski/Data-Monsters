# 6) 12-Month Projections & Loan Repayment Coverage

## Projection Assumptions

To provide a conservative but lender-friendly outlook, the following assumptions are used:

- Active client growth: ramp to **25–35 clients** by month 12
- Average recurring revenue per client blended across segments
- One-time setup fees collected on new accounts
- Gradual increase in operating expenses as client count expands

## Target Run-Rate by Month 12

- **Active clients:** 25–35
- **Monthly recurring revenue (MRR):** $15,000–$20,000
- **Annualized recurring run-rate:** $180,000–$240,000

## Illustrative Month-12 Revenue Mix

| Revenue Component | Conservative Case | Growth Case |
|---|---:|---:|
| Recurring subscriptions (MRR) | $15,000 | $20,000 |
| Setup fees (monthly average at scale) | $4,000 | $6,000 |
| Add-on/custom work | $1,000 | $2,000 |
| **Total monthly revenue** | **$20,000** | **$28,000** |

## Cash Flow and Repayment Logic

The model supports repayment through recurring cash flow rather than one-off projects:

- MRR compounds as clients are added and retained
- Setup fees improve near-term cash flow during growth
- Overhead remains relatively low compared with revenue potential
- Contingency reserve reduces default risk during ramp periods

Even in a conservative case, projected monthly revenue at scale provides room for debt service while continuing operations and reinvestment.
